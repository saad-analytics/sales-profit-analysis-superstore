# Project Case Study: Sales & Profit Strategy Analysis for a Retail Superstore

**Analyst:** Saad
**Tools:** Python, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib
**Dataset:** 10,000+ transactions across multiple product categories

---

## The Problem

The business was generating millions in revenue but operating blind. They had no clear answers on:

- Why some high-sales months resulted in lower bank balances
- Which products were actually draining their resources
- Whether their aggressive discount strategies were sustainable or just eating their margins

---

## What I Did

**Data Architecture**
Cleaned and standardized a messy dataset of 10,000+ rows to ensure 100% accuracy in reporting.

**Performance Metrics**
Built a custom analysis for Monthly Revenue, Profit Margins, Average Order Value (AOV), and Shipping Efficiency.

**Leak Identification**
Conducted a deep-dive into Discount Traps and shipping costs to find where profit was being lost.

**Customer Segmentation**
Classified the customer base into VIPs and Occasional Buyers to help prioritize marketing spend.

---

## Key Findings

### The Furniture Profit Leak
While Furniture looked successful due to high sales, it had near-zero margins (2.5%). Tables and Bookcases were actively losing money.

### The November Trap
Record-high sales in November actually produced less profit than December due to excessive discounting.

### VIP Customer Discovery
The top 10% of customers provide almost half of the company's total profit — requiring a complete shift in retention strategy.

---

## Strategic Reallocation Plan

| Priority | Recommendation | Rationale |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| 🛑 STOP | Heavy discounting on Furniture | Near-zero margins make this unsustainable |
| 📉 REDUCE | November discount campaigns | High sales masking low profit |
| 🚀 SCALE | VIP customer retention | Top 10% drive 50% of profit |
| 📈 INVESTIGATE | Tables and Bookcases | Actively losing money despite sales volume |

---

## Business Conclusion

This analysis gave the business a clear path to protect margins and redirect resources toward what actually drives profit — not just revenue.
